Operator Guidelines

Follow these essential safety practices on every job site.

Pre-Operation Inspection

Walk around the machine. Check fluid levels, tires/tracks, lights, hydraulics, and safety decals before starting.

Wear Proper PPE

Hard hat, high-vis vest, steel-toe boots, gloves, and hearing protection are mandatory on all job sites.

Know Load Limits

Never exceed rated capacity. Factor in attachments, terrain, and dynamic loads. When in doubt, ask.

Hazard Awareness

Watch for overhead power lines, underground utilities, uneven ground, and nearby workers. Mark the zone.

Fuel & Shutdown

Shut down completely before refueling. No smoking. Let the engine cool before checking fluids or storing.

Report Incidents

Report all near misses, damage, or injuries immediately. Call our 24/7 safety line — we're here to help.

Our Process

Pre-Rental Inspection

Every machine goes through a rigorous 37-point check before it leaves our yard.

01

Fluid & Mechanical Check

Oil, coolant, hydraulic fluid, and fuel levels are checked and topped off. Belts, hoses, and filters are inspected for wear. Any component below spec is replaced before rental.

  • Engine oil & coolant levels
  • Hydraulic system pressure test
  • Air filter & fuel filter condition
02

Safety Systems & Decals

All safety guards, lights, backup alarms, and decals are verified. Seat belts, ROPS structures, and fire extinguishers are confirmed present and functional.

  • Backup alarm & beacon lights
  • Seat belt & ROPS integrity
  • Safety decals & emergency shut-off
03

Operational Test Run

Every machine is started, cycled through all functions, and test-driven. Controls, gauges, and performance are verified before the unit is cleared for rental.

  • Full cycle test on all controls
  • Gauge & dashboard verification
  • Brake & steering response check

On Site

Know Your Surroundings

Different job sites come with different hazards. Stay alert and stay safe.

Construction

Overhead hazards, moving vehicles, and uneven terrain are the top risks on active construction sites.

Road Work

Traffic control, flaggers, and high-vis PPE are critical. Keep equipment away from live lanes.

Demolition

Structural instability, dust, flying debris, and buried utilities make demolition especially high-risk.

Excavation

Cave-ins, struck-by, and hazardous atmospheres. Always use trench boxes and monitor air quality.

Warehouse

Pedestrian traffic, racking stability, and blind corners. Use spotters and keep speeds low.

Night Work

Reduced visibility, fatigue, and cold weather risks. Ensure all lights and reflective gear are on.
